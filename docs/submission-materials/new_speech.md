# Neural ERP — Pitch Script (5-6 Minutes)

**Event:** Pragati 2026 | St. Francis Institute of Technology
**Speakers:** Aditya Soni (Speaker 1) | Riddhi Patil (Speaker 2) | Mariam Badure (Speaker 3)
**Tone:** Conversational, bold, confident

---

## SPEAKER 1 — ADITYA (~2 minutes)

### Slide 1 — Title + Hook (15s)

"Good morning.

Here's a number that should bother everyone in this room — 83% of India's engineering graduates from the Class of 2024 did not receive a single job or internship offer. Eighty-three percent.

I'm Aditya Soni. And we built Neural ERP to fix that."

### Slide 2 — The Problem (25s)

"Here's why this is happening.

India has over 52,000 colleges. And the average college runs 5 to 15 completely disconnected software tools — one for attendance, another for exams, a separate portal for placements, another for fees. None of them talk to each other.

Administrators waste 10 to 15 hours every single week just re-entering the same data across systems. There's zero career support built into any of these tools. No dropout predictions. No early warnings. Parents spend 5 to 15 lakh on education and get zero real-time visibility.

The infrastructure is broken. And students are paying the price."

### Slide 3 — Market Opportunity (25s)

"Now, this broken infrastructure sits inside a massive market.

India's EdTech sector is valued at 7.5 billion dollars today and is projected to hit 20 billion dollars by 2030 — that's a 20% compound annual growth rate. And the global Education ERP market alone is crossing 16 billion dollars.

There are over 52,000 colleges, 1,300+ universities, and 46 million students enrolled right now. And with NEP 2020 mandating digital transformation and NAAC requiring data-driven documentation — every single college now *needs* a modern ERP.

Our total addressable market — all education ERP in India — is 3,000 crore rupees. Our serviceable market — 5,000 engineering and management colleges actively seeking digital transformation — is 350 crore. And we're targeting 200 paying institutions in five years at an average of 7.5 lakh per year — that's a 15 crore obtainable market."

### Slide 4 — Target Market (20s)

"Our buyers are principals, deans, and Training & Placement Officers in Tier 2 and Tier 3 engineering and management colleges. These are the 3,000+ institutions where the placement crisis is worst — where 45 to 60% of graduates leave without a job.

These decision-makers are judged on two things: NAAC scores and placement rates. We improve both. That's why they'll buy.

Now I'll hand over to Riddhi to show you what we've built."

---

## SPEAKER 2 — RIDDHI (~2 minutes)

### Slide 5 — The Solution (20s)

"Neural ERP replaces that entire mess with one AI-powered platform.

We don't just digitize — we add intelligence. Our system auto-captures classroom content through Smart Board OCR, personalizes learning paths using machine learning, predicts which students are at risk of dropping out or failing placements — and then actually connects them with opportunities through our Career Intelligence Engine.

Parents get real-time updates on fees, attendance, and results — directly on WhatsApp. No app downloads, no portals to remember. Just WhatsApp.

All of this, secured with 3-factor authentication, AES-256 encryption, and blockchain-verified certificates."

### Slide 6 — 14 Modules, One Platform (20s)

"The platform has 14 integrated modules and over 250 features.

The Academic Core handles attendance, timetables, exams, assignments, library, and fees — with bulletproof fee management that auto-carries-forward balances, supports UPI, wallets, and sends payment links via WhatsApp. The Intelligence Layer adds predictive analytics, an AI chatbot, career matching, and natural language queries — ask 'show me students below 75% attendance in Class 10' instead of navigating five menus.

One login. One platform. Everything connected. And it works offline — with auto-sync when you're back online."

### Slide 7 — Competitive Advantage (25s)

"We studied every major ERP in this space — globally and in India. Here's what we found.

Ellucian Banner powers 1,500 universities worldwide — but users call its interface 'lipstick on a pig.' Their own customer support is described as 'awful.' PowerSchool dominates K-12 in the US — but has documented cases of wiping user data on page refresh. Classe365 claims to be all-in-one, but has performance lags, API throttling, and bait-and-switch pricing complaints. Fedena — India's popular open-source option — has broken fee management, no offline mode, security concerns, and an abandoned community edition.

Every single competitor has outdated UI, zero AI, zero WhatsApp integration, and zero offline capability. That's the gap we're filling."

### Slide 8 — Differentiators (15s)

"Four reasons we win.

First — modern, mobile-first design. Every competitor is criticized for clunky, outdated interfaces. We built for 2026, not 2006.

Second — AI-native from day one. We predict outcomes before they happen. Competitors only report what already went wrong.

Third — WhatsApp-native communication. Fee reminders, attendance alerts, exam results — sent where parents already are. No competitor does this.

Fourth — founders build the product. Our cost base is 50 to 70% lower than anyone in this space, and our pricing is fully transparent — no hidden fees, no surprises.

Mariam will now walk you through the business model."

## SPEAKER 3 — MARIAM (~2 minutes)

### Slide 9 — Revenue Model (25s)

"Our revenue engine is built on two pillars: storage-based pricing and premium plans.

Every institution starts on a base tier with core modules and standard cloud storage. As their data grows — student records, lecture content from Smart Board OCR, analytics history — they scale into higher storage tiers. Premium plans unlock advanced AI features: predictive analytics dashboards, career intelligence, blockchain certificates, and priority support.

This creates natural, usage-driven revenue growth. The more a college uses Neural ERP, the more value they get — and the more they pay.

On top of this, we have secondary streams: a freemium career module at 499 rupees per month per student, recruiter partnerships, placement success fees, and consulting services."

### Slide 10 — Financials & Funding Ask (20s)

"We need exactly 12 lakh rupees in total external funding. That's it. That's the entire ask.

Stage zero — we've already built the MVP on 1 lakh of personal savings. Stage one — 12 lakh from friends, family, and a small angel round for sales, cloud infrastructure, and operations. Stage two — we self-fund entirely from revenue. No VC dependency. No unnecessary dilution.

We hit breakeven at Month 27 with 54 lakh in revenue and a 35% margin. By Month 42, we project 3.75 crore revenue at a 73% profit margin."

### Slide 11 — Roadmap (15s)

"Phase 1 — Years 1 and 2: 20 colleges, MVP to full product, first paying customers.

Phase 2 — Year 3: 60 colleges, mobile apps live, full AI capabilities deployed.

Phase 3 — Years 4 and 5: 200+ colleges, enterprise B2B deals, and international expansion."

### Slide 12 — Vision & Close (20s)

"By 2030, Neural ERP will power over 1,000 institutions and impact more than 10 million students every single year.

We're not building another admin tool. We're building the operating system that turns colleges into career launchpads.

Remember that 83% number from the beginning? We exist to bring it down.

*From Enrollment to Employment — Neural ERP.*

Thank you. We're ready for your questions."

## Quick Rehearsal Notes

- **Total spoken words:** ~950 (targets 6-6.5 min at natural pace)
- Speak slowly on numbers — let stats land
- Pause after the 83% opening hook — let it sink in
- On the competitor slide — say "lipstick on a pig" and "wiping user data" with conviction. These are real user quotes, not opinions. Let them land.
- Emphasize "every single competitor" during the gap summary — sweep your hand across the slide
- On the funding ask, say "12 lakh" with confidence — the low number is a strength
- Practice the closing callback to the 83% stat — it creates a powerful loop
- Eye contact with judges during the competitive advantage and funding slides
- The WhatsApp point is the crowd-pleaser — deliver it with a slight smile, it's common sense that nobody else has done
